Supplements Guide

Supporting your ascent with science-backed supplementation

← Back to Nutrition

⚠️ Important Disclaimer

Supplements are not required or necessary for your fitness journey. The foundation of your success comes from consistent training, proper nutrition, adequate sleep, and dedication. Supplements can serve as tools to support and enhance your progress, but they should never replace the fundamentals. Think of them as optional aids that can help you ascend further, not requirements for growth. Always consult with a healthcare professional before starting any supplement regimen, especially if you have pre-existing health conditions or are taking medications.

Understanding Supplements

Supplements can complement your training and nutrition, but they work best when used strategically alongside a solid foundation. Below, we've outlined key supplements that may support various aspects of your fitness journey, from performance enhancement to recovery and overall health.

Performance & Strength

Creatine Monohydrate

Purpose: Enhances strength, power output, and muscle mass gains.

Benefits: Increases ATP production, improves high-intensity performance, supports muscle growth, and may enhance cognitive function.

Whey Protein

Purpose: Convenient source of high-quality protein for muscle repair and growth.

Benefits: Fast-digesting protein, complete amino acid profile, supports muscle protein synthesis, convenient post-workout option.

HMB (Beta-Hydroxy Beta-Methylbutyrate)

Purpose: May reduce muscle protein breakdown during intense training.

Benefits: Supports muscle preservation, may aid recovery, potentially helpful during cutting phases or high-volume training.

BCAAs (Branched-Chain Amino Acids)

Purpose: Essential amino acids that support muscle protein synthesis and reduce fatigue.

Benefits: May reduce exercise-induced fatigue, supports muscle recovery, can be useful during fasted training.

Beta-Alanine

Purpose: Increases muscle carnosine levels to buffer acid during high-intensity exercise.

Benefits: Improves endurance, delays muscle fatigue, enhances performance in high-rep training, works best with consistent use.

Citrulline Malate

Purpose: Enhances nitric oxide production and blood flow.

Benefits: Improves exercise performance, increases blood flow to muscles, reduces fatigue, enhances "pump" during training.

Vitamins & Minerals

Multivitamins

Purpose: Comprehensive nutrient support to fill potential dietary gaps.

Benefits: Ensures adequate micronutrient intake, supports overall health, convenient insurance policy for nutrient deficiencies.

Vitamin B Complex (B12, Folate, B6)

Purpose: Essential for energy metabolism and nervous system function.

Benefits: Supports energy production, aids in red blood cell formation, important for cognitive function, especially important for active individuals.

Vitamin D3

Purpose: Critical for bone health, immune function, and muscle function.

Benefits: Supports calcium absorption, important for bone density, may support immune health, many people are deficient, especially in winter months.

Magnesium

Purpose: Essential mineral involved in hundreds of biochemical reactions.

Benefits: Supports muscle function and relaxation, aids sleep quality, important for energy production, many athletes may be deficient.

Zinc

Purpose: Important for immune function, protein synthesis, and hormone production.

Benefits: Supports immune health, aids in recovery, important for testosterone production, may be depleted through intense training.

Omega-3 & Brain Health

Omega-3 Fatty Acids (DHA/EPA)

Purpose: Essential fats that support brain health, inflammation management, and cardiovascular health.

Benefits: Reduces inflammation, supports brain function, important for heart health, may aid recovery, most people don't get enough from diet alone.

Phosphatidylserine

Purpose: Phospholipid that supports brain health and may reduce exercise-induced stress.

Benefits: May reduce cortisol levels, supports cognitive function, may aid recovery from intense training.

Lion's Mane Mushroom

Purpose: Medicinal mushroom that supports cognitive function and brain health.

Benefits: May support memory and focus, supports nerve growth factor, may aid in cognitive performance, natural nootropic.

Hormone & Energy Support

Acetyl-L-Carnitine

Purpose: Amino acid derivative that supports energy production and brain function.

Benefits: Aids in fat metabolism, supports cognitive function, may enhance energy levels, supports mitochondrial function.

Fenugreek

Purpose: Herb that may support testosterone levels and libido.

Benefits: May support natural testosterone production, traditional use for male health, may support strength and muscle mass.

Tongkat Ali

Purpose: Traditional herb that may support testosterone and energy levels.

Benefits: May support free testosterone levels, may reduce stress hormones, traditional use for male vitality and performance.

Ashwagandha

Purpose: Adaptogenic herb that supports stress management and may support hormone health.

Benefits: Reduces cortisol levels, may support testosterone, improves stress resilience, supports recovery and sleep quality.

Boron

Purpose: Trace mineral that may support testosterone and bone health.

Benefits: May support free testosterone levels, important for bone health, supports vitamin D metabolism.

D-Aspartic Acid

Purpose: Amino acid that may support testosterone production.

Benefits: May support natural testosterone synthesis, may aid in strength and muscle gains, especially in younger individuals.

Remember: The Foundation First

Supplements can be valuable tools in your journey, but they work best when built on a foundation of consistent training, proper nutrition, adequate sleep, and unwavering dedication. Focus on mastering the fundamentals first—supplements are the cherry on top, not the foundation. Your ascent is built through discipline and hard work, with supplements serving as optional support along the way.
